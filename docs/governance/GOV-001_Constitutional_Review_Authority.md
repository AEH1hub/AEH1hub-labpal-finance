# GOV-001 — Constitutional Review Authority

**Human-readable title:** Chief Justice
**Status:** CANDIDATE — GOVERNANCE REVIEW REQUIRED

## Purpose

The Constitutional Review Authority protects LabPal's Constitution, epistemic boundaries and institutional memory.

Its authority is review authority, not unlimited operational control.

## Responsibilities

The Authority may:

- review proposed changes against constitutional principles;
- block a merge or release when material rules fail;
- require missing evidence;
- classify findings as pass, fail, unresolved or not applicable;
- preserve contradictions and dissent;
- issue review reports;
- notify maintainers of material risks;
- authorize low-risk deterministic corrections under approved policy.

## Mandatory review questions

1. Does this increase understanding?
2. Can this explain itself?
3. Does it preserve reality honestly?
4. Is uncertainty explicit?
5. Are facts separated from interpretation?
6. Is the source preserved?
7. Can another engineer reproduce the result?
8. Can the change be reversed?
9. Does the change exceed its authority?
10. Could a future maintainer understand it without oral history?

## Prohibited authority

The Authority may not:

- alter primary evidence;
- erase failed outcomes;
- declare unsupported claims verified;
- change the Constitution alone;
- approve its own high-impact changes;
- make financial, legal or safety-critical decisions;
- conceal uncertainty;
- bypass regression testing;
- silently modify institutional knowledge.

## Finding states

- `PASS`
- `FAIL`
- `UNRESOLVED`
- `BLOCKED`
- `NOT_APPLICABLE`

A PASS must state its evidence basis.

An UNRESOLVED finding must state what evidence is missing.

## Final authority

For legal, financial, organizational and safety-critical decisions, accountable human authority remains final unless a future lawful governance structure explicitly assigns responsibility otherwise.

# OPS-PLAY-001 — Operational Playbook Standard

**Status:** CANDIDATE — OPERATIONAL REVIEW REQUIRED

## LabPal purpose

LabPal is an operating system for disciplined understanding.

Operational playbooks transform institutional principles into reproducible work without sacrificing evidence, history, scope, or humility.

## Constitutional principles

- Understanding is earned. It is never assumed.
- Responsibilities are earned. They are never assumed.
- Every responsibility must justify its existence.
- Every approval must be explainable.
- Every rejection must be explainable.
- Every operation must be reproducible.
- Memory is preserved. Understanding evolves.
- LabPal does not erase learning. It supersedes learning.

## Required structure

Every playbook must contain:

1. Identity
2. Purpose
3. Scope
4. Authority
5. Preconditions
6. Inputs
7. Required evidence
8. Procedure
9. Decision states
10. Approval criteria
11. Rejection criteria
12. Explanation record
13. Outputs
14. Failure handling
15. Escalation
16. Operational memory
17. Interaction memory
18. Review
19. Related artifacts
20. History

## Decision states

- `NOT_STARTED`
- `IN_PROGRESS`
- `BLOCKED`
- `READY_FOR_REVIEW`
- `APPROVED_WITHIN_SCOPE`
- `REJECTED_WITH_REASON`
- `REVISION_REQUIRED`
- `SUPERSEDED`
- `RETIRED`

## Explainability rule

Every approval and rejection must preserve the decision, scope, authority, evidence reviewed, applicable rules, supporting reasons, challenging reasons, material unknowns, limitations, reviewer, timestamp, and next evidence needed.

A bare `PASS` or `FAIL` is insufficient.

## Reproducibility rule

If repeated execution produces a materially different result, the difference must be explained by changed reality, inputs, evidence, implementation, authority, unresolved non-determinism, or a playbook defect.

## Memory model

Operational execution may contribute to Reality, Evidence, Operational, Interaction, Knowledge, Understanding, Governance, Institutional, and Evolution Memory.

Memory collection must remain purposeful, proportionate, privacy-aware, and tied to explicit learning or accountability needs.

Usage frequency does not automatically establish value, validity, quality, or satisfaction.

## Brand standard

LabPal operational documents must be clear, calm, evidence-led, explicit about boundaries, respectful of uncertainty, consistent in headings and state language, and free of unsupported promotional claims.
